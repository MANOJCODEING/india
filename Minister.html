Narendra Modi – Prime Minister of India Narendra Modi, the 14th and current Prime Minister of India, is one of the most influential leaders in Indian history. Born on 17th September 1950 in Vadnagar, Gujarat, he rose from humble beginnings, famously working as a tea seller in his early life. Modi began his political journey with the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) and later joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). As the Chief Minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014, he was praised for his focus on economic development and governance. Since becoming Prime Minister in 2014, he has launched several major initiatives like Make in India, Digital India, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, and Startup India. He is known for bold decisions such as the abrogation of Article 370, demonetization, and introducing the Goods and Services Tax (GST). Internationally, he has strengthened India’s image through active diplomacy. Narendra Modi is seen as a symbol of strong leadership and vision for a new India.


Droupadi Murmu – President of India Droupadi Murmu is the 15th and current President of India, and the first tribal woman to hold the highest constitutional post in the country. Born on 20th June 1958 in Mayurbhanj, Odisha, she belongs to the Santhal tribal community. Before entering national politics, she worked as a teacher and later served as a minister in the Odisha government. She was also the Governor of Jharkhand from 2015 to 2021, becoming the first woman to hold that position. In 2022, she made history by becoming the first tribal and second woman President of India. Known for her simplicity, humility, and dedication to public service, Droupadi Murmu represents the voice of marginalized communities in India. Her election is considered a significant step forward for social inclusion and women empowerment. As President, she plays a vital role in upholding the Constitution and representing the unity and diversity of India on the global stage.


Nirmala Sitharaman – Finance Minister of India Nirmala Sitharaman is the current Finance Minister of India, serving since May 2019, and is the first full-time woman to hold this position. She was born on August 18, 1959, in Tamil Nadu and has a background in economics. Before becoming Finance Minister, she also served as the Defence Minister, becoming the second woman after Indira Gandhi to hold that post. Known for her strong leadership and clear vision, she has played a crucial role in shaping India’s economic policies, especially during challenging times like the COVID-19 pandemic. Under her leadership, several reforms were introduced to boost manufacturing, digital economy, startups, and tax policies. She is also an active member of the Rajya Sabha. Nirmala Sitharaman’s journey is an inspiration for women in politics and public service, as she continues to be a key figure in India’s economic growth and international financial diplomacy.


Rajnath Singh – Defence Minister of India Rajnath Singh is the current Defence Minister of India, a position he has held since May 2019. Born on July 10, 1951, in Uttar Pradesh, he is one of the senior-most leaders of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). With decades of experience in Indian politics, Rajnath Singh has served as the Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh, Union Minister of Home Affairs, and President of the BJP. As Defence Minister, he has focused on strengthening India’s military forces, promoting indigenous defence production, and enhancing national security. Under his leadership, major defence deals have been signed, and initiatives like Atmanirbhar Bharat in Defence have gained momentum. He is known for his firm stance on national defence and has represented India on global military and strategic platforms. Rajnath Singh continues to play a key role in building a modern, strong, and self-reliant Indian defence system.


Jagdeep Dhankhar – Vice President of India Jagdeep Dhankhar is the 14th Vice President of India, having assumed office on August 11, 2022. Born on May 18, 1951, in Kithana village, Rajasthan, Dhankhar comes from a humble farming background. Despite early struggles, he pursued higher education in physics and law, eventually establishing a successful legal career. He served as a senior advocate in the Supreme Court of India and gained a reputation for his sharp legal acumen. Jagdeep Dhankhar began his political journey in the late 1980s. He was elected to the Lok Sabha from Jhunjhunu, Rajasthan, in 1989, and served as Minister of State for Parliamentary Affairs in the Chandra Shekhar government (1990–1991). Over the years, he has been associated with various political parties but has been a key figure in the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) since the early 2000s. Before becoming Vice President, he served as the Governor of West Bengal from 2019 to 2022, where he gained national attention for his frequent confrontations with the state government. His tenure as governor was marked by his strong emphasis on constitutional values and his assertive stance on governance issues. As Vice President, Jagdeep Dhankhar also serves as the ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha, where he is responsible for ensuring the smooth functioning of India’s Upper House of Parliament. He is known for his disciplined and law-focused approach to parliamentary procedures and his commitment to upholding democratic principles. Jagdeep Dhankhar’s journey from a rural boy to one of the highest constitutional positions in the country is an inspiring example of determination, education, and public service. He continues to play an essential role in shaping India’s democratic discourse with dignity and integrity.


Ashwini Vaishnaw – Minister of Railways, Communications & IT Ashwini Vaishnaw is the current Union Minister for Railways, Communications, and Electronics & Information Technology in India. He was born on July 18, 1970, in Jodhpur, Rajasthan. Vaishnaw is known for his strong academic and administrative background. He holds an engineering degree from MBM Engineering College and later completed his MBA from Wharton School, USA. He joined the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) in 1994 and served in Odisha and at the central level, handling various key roles. After resigning from civil services, he worked in multinational companies like GE and Siemens, and later started his own business in infrastructure development. In 2019, he was elected to the Rajya Sabha from Odisha, representing the BJP. As a minister, Vaishnaw has introduced major reforms in Indian Railways, including the launch of Vande Bharat trains and station modernization.
